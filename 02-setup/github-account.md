# GitHub Account

## What is it?
A GitHub account is your online identity in the developer world. It is where you store your repositories, collaborate with others, and showcase your work.

## Why do we use it?
An account is required to upload (push) code to GitHub, contribute to open-source projects, and interact with other developers via issues or pull requests. It acts as your digital resume.

## Basic Syntax
There is no command line syntax for this. It is done entirely in the browser.

## Example
1. Go to [github.com](https://github.com/).
2. Click **Sign up**.
3. Choose a professional username (e.g., your real name or a recognizable handle).
4. Enter your email and a strong password.
5. Verify your email address.

## Common Mistakes
- **Using unprofessional usernames:** `cool_gamer_99` is not great for a professional portfolio. Stick to combinations of your name.
- **Losing access:** Always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) to secure your account.

## Quick Summary
- Sign up at github.com.
- Choose a professional username.
- Enable 2FA for security.
- This is your digital developer resume!
